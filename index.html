PhD Candidate in Information Systems and Analytics

Xiaoxuan (Cleo) Xu

I am a PhD candidate in Business Administration with a concentration in Information Systems and Analytics at Stevens Institute of Technology. My research uses computational social science, natural language processing, and large language models, paired with human-centered analysis, to study how digital communities communicate, organize, and make meaning.

Before my PhD, I trained in media, culture, and communication at New York University and advertising at Minzu University of China, with prior industry experience in publishing, marketing, and financial news. Outside academia, cats, sports, and movies keep me grounded.

Research

My work studies how people, organizations, and communities create cultural meaning, coordinate action, and communicate in platform-mediated social and economic life. I use state-of-the-art computational methods to examine how discourse, narratives, and collective signals emerge from large-scale digital traces and inform organizational decisions, market dynamics, and societal understanding.

Online Communities and Platforms

Group dynamics, content moderation, and platform governance.

Multimodal Digital Discourse

Podcasts, audio narratives, text, and computational approaches to culture and storytelling.

Computational Social Science

NLP, LLMs, embeddings, causal inference, econometrics, and network analysis.

Papers

The Deplatforming Paradox

Xiaoxuan Xu, Jingyi Sun, Feng Mai

Major revision at MIS Quarterly. Presented at INFORMS 2023.

Examines hidden bias spillovers from deplatformed communities using word embedding association methods.

A Podcast Analytics Approach for Decoding Entrepreneurial Storytelling

Xiaoxuan Xu, Jingyi Sun, Feng Mai

Presented at WITS 2025 and Computational Methods in Management Research Workshop 2026.

Develops an LLM-based rhetorical structure parsing pipeline to quantify narrative structures from podcast audio.

When Communities Watch: Locational Public Discourse and Municipal Bond Markets

Xiaoxuan Xu, Jingyi Sun, Feng Mai

Work in progress.

Links geo-locational community discourse to municipal fiscal health through NLP-extracted soft information.
